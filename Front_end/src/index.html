PM-Kishan

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana, commonly known as PM-Kisan, is a government scheme launched by the Government of India in 2019. The scheme aims to provide financial assistance to small and marginal farmers in India, by providing them with a minimum income support of Rs. 6,000 per year.

Under the PM-Kisan scheme, eligible farmers receive direct cash transfers of Rs. 2,000 each, three times a year, directly into their bank accounts. The scheme is aimed at providing financial assistance to farmers for their agricultural needs, including purchasing seeds, fertilizers, and other necessary inputs.

To be eligible for the PM-Kisan scheme, farmers must have less than 2 hectares of cultivable land, and must be able to provide proof of ownership of the land. The scheme is also open to certain categories of farmers who do not own land, such as sharecroppers and tenant farmers.

The PM-Kisan scheme is a part of the government's larger effort to improve the welfare of farmers in India, and to address the issue of agrarian distress in the country.

Digital Gujarat

Digital Gujarat is an initiative launched by the Government of Gujarat to provide various government services to citizens online. The aim of the initiative is to make it easier for citizens to access government services from anywhere, and at any time, through a single digital platform.

The Digital Gujarat platform offers a wide range of services to citizens, including access to government documents such as birth certificates, death certificates, and marriage certificates, as well as application for various government schemes and services such as ration cards, income certificates, and driving licenses. Citizens can also use the platform to make online payments for government services, such as payment of utility bills and taxes, and to access various government portals such as the e-Dhara land records portal and the e-Stamping portal.

The Digital Gujarat initiative has helped to streamline government services and reduce the time and effort required by citizens to access them. It has also helped to reduce corruption and improve transparency in government processes.

Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao

"Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao" (Save the Daughter, Educate the Daughter) is a flagship campaign launched by the Government of India in 2015. The aim of the campaign is to address the issue of declining female child sex ratio in India, and to promote education and empowerment of girls.

The campaign seeks to create awareness about the importance of saving and educating the girl child, and to encourage the adoption of positive practices towards women and girls in Indian society. It is based on a three-pronged strategy of prevention of gender-biased sex selection, protection of girls, and education and empowerment of girls.

The Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao campaign includes various initiatives such as awareness campaigns, community mobilization, training and capacity building, and monitoring and evaluation. The campaign also includes various schemes and programs aimed at empowering and educating girls, such as scholarships, vocational training, and financial assistance for higher education.

The Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao campaign has helped to raise awareness about the issue of gender inequality in India, and has contributed to a significant increase in the number of girls enrolling in schools and colleges across the country. It has also helped to promote positive attitudes towards women and girls, and to reduce gender-based discrimination and violence.

SSIP

The State Sector Investment Programme (SSIP) is a scheme launched by the Government of India to provide financial assistance to state governments for the implementation of various development projects. The programme aims to support state governments in improving their infrastructure and services, and in promoting economic growth and development.

Under the SSIP, the central government provides financial assistance to state governments in the form of grants and loans for the implementation of various projects in sectors such as transportation, energy, water supply, and sanitation, among others. The programme is designed to provide flexibility to state governments in deciding their own priorities for development and in implementing projects that are tailored to their specific needs.

The SSIP is an important component of the overall development strategy of the Government of India, and is aimed at promoting inclusive and sustainable growth across all states and regions of the country. The programme has helped to improve the quality of infrastructure and services in many states, and has contributed to the overall economic development of the country.

Overall, the SSIP is an important initiative that aims to support state governments in their efforts to promote growth and development, and to create a more equitable and prosperous society for all citizens.

PMMY

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) is a flagship scheme launched by the Government of India in 2015 to provide financial support to micro and small enterprises (MSEs). The scheme provides loans up to Rs. 10 lakh to these enterprises through a network of partner institutions, such as banks, non-banking financial companies (NBFCs), and microfinance institutions (MFIs).

PMMY aims to promote entrepreneurship and self-employment among individuals from low-income backgrounds and to help them start and grow their own businesses. The scheme provides loans in three categories, namely Shishu, Kishor, and Tarun, based on the size and stage of the business.

The Shishu category covers loans up to Rs. 50,000, while the Kishor category covers loans between Rs. 50,000 and Rs. 5 lakh, and the Tarun category covers loans between Rs. 5 lakh and Rs. 10 lakh. The loans are provided without any collateral security or guarantor, making it easier for MSEs to access credit.

PMMY has been successful in promoting entrepreneurship and self-employment, with over 30 million loans disbursed under the scheme since its launch. It has also contributed to the growth of the MSME sector in India and has helped to create employment opportunities for millions of people.

Overall, PMMY is an important initiative of the Government of India aimed at promoting entrepreneurship and self-employment among individuals from low-income backgrounds, and has been instrumental in promoting economic growth and development in the country.

SCSS

Senior Citizen Saving Scheme (SCSS) is a savings scheme introduced by the Government of India for individuals above 60 years of age. The scheme provides a regular source of income to senior citizens and offers an attractive rate of interest.

Under the SCSS, individuals can invest a maximum of Rs. 15 lakh, and the interest rate is revised every quarter. The current interest rate is 7.4% per annum. The scheme has a tenure of 5 years, which can be extended by another 3 years.

The SCSS provides several benefits to senior citizens, including regular income, high returns, tax benefits, and low risk. The scheme is also backed by the Government of India, which makes it a safe and secure investment option for senior citizens.

To invest in the SCSS, individuals need to have a savings account in a post office or a bank. They also need to provide proof of age and identity, such as a passport, driving license, or Aadhaar card.

Overall, the Senior Citizen Saving Scheme is an excellent investment option for senior citizens who are looking for a safe and secure way to invest their savings and earn a regular income. The scheme offers attractive returns and several benefits, making it an ideal choice for individuals above 60 years of age.
